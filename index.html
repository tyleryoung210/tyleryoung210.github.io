Hello, and welcome to my website!

If I had to describe myself in two words, I would probably say passionate and creative.

My passion is expressed in many facets beyond computer science, whether it be in aesthetics, sports teams I support, or music artists I listen to. People who know me know that I love anything to do with adidas, Manchester City, or Donald Glover for example.

I say creative mostly because I like considering things from different perspectives. I wrote a 4000 word essay in high school about the first Macintosh from a historical perspective, and something that resonates with me with the research from the essay is the motto "Think Different." While in some ways it can be a cliche quote, it still holds value, especially with me. Being "basic" is an adjective I do not want to be known as, but rather, "interesting."

Career wise, I aspire to be a software developer once I graduate in May 2020 with an awesome company. Right now my current interests are in both front-end and back-end software development, whether it be with a website, app, or even new stuff like with mixed reality. Right now, I am in this phase of trying out anything I am interested in, and I look forward to continuing this journey!

Involvement

Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers

I have been an active member in SASE since September 2016. I am currently the secretary for the chapter at Virginia Tech and have previously held a freshman representative position. I redesigned how certain tasks are done and created new tools to help the officer board.

Hackers@VT

I just became a member of Hackers@VT this year and have gone to many of their events with companies as well as their Build-To-Learn Hackathon they hosted in the fall semester. I recently became the webmaster for them. I am currently developing their website, and will continue to maintain and oversee it.

Undergraduate Teaching Assistant

This semester, I am a UTA for Virginia Tech's CS 1064 class, Intro to Programming in Python. I work with the instructor, Dr. Bart, and the other TA's to help out students in this class and make sure everything runs smoothly. My contributions include helping out students in lecture, holding office hours, and grading programing assignments.

Council for the Advancement of Minority Engineering Organizations

I am SASE's representative for CAMEO, the umbrella organiation for SASE. I was a part of the first CAMEO Committee, a group of students that helped organize the 2018 CAMEO CareerFest, Virgina Tech's Spring Engineering Career Fair. I helped organize the volunteering for it, from creating the sign-ups to helping out with volunteering on the day of the career fair.
